Get Involved

One of the most important steps toward bridging the digital divide is taking initiative to expand the access of knowledge and digital skills for underserved communities - through accessible workshops, free-to-use resources like computers, and more. As Francine Katsoudas writes via the We Forum , "Digital skills training is the key that unlocks the potential connectivity holds." When those with the means step up to teach others, we help spread essential technical knowledge - knowledge that is critical across many job sectors in today’s world.


Francine also emphasizes, "Digital access and skills — for all — are fundamental to participating in our societies and economies. "

Your action today can make a real difference. Call your Congressperson and demand greater efforts toward digital access. This might include supporting organizations that offer digital skills training in under-resourced communities, fundraising for public computers in spaces like libraries, or launching new initiatives of your own.
