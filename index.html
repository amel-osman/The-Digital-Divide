What exactly is the digital divide?

The digital divide refers to the gap between individuals and communities who do and do not have access to modern technology and information, such as the internet and computers.

This divide can be influenced by factors such as geography, income, education, and disability, leading to unequal access to opportunities in education, employment, healthcare, and more.

As technology becomes increasingly integral to daily life, the digital divide can deepen existing disparities. Bridging this gap is essential for ensuring equitable access to the benefits of digital technology, allowing everyone to fully participate in the digital world.
